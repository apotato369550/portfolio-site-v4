Who are we?

You’ve probably heard of the name Google. If you own a smartphone or a computer, and have a stable internet connection, you’ve likely used one of its tools: the classic Google search, Gmail, Google Drive, Google Docs, and so much more, with their use almost disappearing into everyday life.

What’s less obvious is that the people learning how these things are built, maintained, and used responsibly can be found a lot closer than expected. Who could have thought that the people on the other side of these tools can be found at your local university?

We are Google Developer Groups on Campus. A global program supported by Google. A non-profit, student-led organization, our chapter duly recognized by the University of San Carlos.

We have served a learning-driven community since its inception, connecting academic knowledge with real-world technology applications, and bridging the gap between what students learn in school and what is actually used in the industry.

As part of an international network with thousands of chapters worldwide, GDGoC-USC operates to serve the passionate local tech community here in Cebu.

Our Vision

To nurture a community of future innovators and leaders who are industry-ready, socially aware, and committed to learning in an evolving technological landscape.

Our Mission

To cultivate a collaborative learning environment that enables individuals to develop both professional competencies and a strong sense of responsibility in using technology to create positive impact.

What do we do?

From the outside, it’s easy to feel confused, or even a little intimidated, by anything that carries the word technology. To onlookers, what we do tends to look very abstract, fast-moving, and larger than it really is.

In practice, the work is much simpler.

At GDGoC-USC, we spend time learning things that don’t normally fit in a classroom setting. We look at tools and how to use them, we break down systems to build them better, and we play with ideas to see how they fit in the broader community. We learn by doing.

We teach each other things we might not already know. And we help demystify tech, one event at a time.

Some of our initiatives include:

  • Hands-on technical workshops where attendees learn and apply in real-time
  • Knowledge-sharing events featuring guest speakers from students adept in their field and industry experts
  • Skill-building initiatives to prepare students to work with and alongside technology

Through these efforts, our members gain industry-relevant experience and lessons, meeting and talking to new people, while learning to lead and innovate.

What happens here?

GDGoc-USC is composed of a diverse and growing community of university students who share an interest in technology and innovation.

The organization maintains a strong membership base each academic year, supported by a team composed of dedicated student-leaders and organizers. These students are exposed to project management, collaboration, and cutting-edge tech to be taught during sessions, fostering leadership, cooperation, and learning.

Becoming a member doesn’t begin with credentials or experience. It usually begins with curiosity and aspiration: wanting to be a software developer, designer, data scientist, or wanting to know more about technology in general.

But what we offer to them is not just access to skill-building events, learning materials, or fun conversations. As time passes, familiar faces become references, with former members graduating and taking up roles in the industry or pursuing further studies.

Over time, curiosity turns to familiarity, and familiarity turns to trust. And that is what gives our community its shape.

What our work looks like

Our work can take on many forms. Some efforts are fully backed and held in venues: carefully planned, running end-to-end. Others are a lot smaller by design: online meetups, focused workshops, or initiatives led by a handful of officers who want to give their take on a topic they’re passionate about. The scale varies, but the intent stays the same.

HOW2PYTHON Series - September 2026 to December 2025

Python - one of the best entry-level programming languages, and often the introduction to programming for a lot of people in this day and age - is taught stemming first from principles so that beginners can follow.

Knowing that Python is essential in an era dominated by data and artificial intelligence, we give our members an edge that they wouldn’t normally get if learning straight from the syllabus.

Code for a Cause - November 2025, in response to Typhoon Tino

Some of our work arrives in response to circumstance. Code for a Cause was organized quickly and was coordinated across multiple student organizations, with our technical skills not as a showcase, but as a means to spread awareness and gather help when those are in need.

Beyond Tutorials & AI on the Cloud - February 2026

Us here at GDGoC understand that you can only learn and do so much through tutorials, and sometimes, with the help of others, new tips, tools, and tricks reveal themselves that would have taken longer to figure out solo. Leveraging real-world datasets and applications, as well as cutting-edge established technologies, we show that there’s more to learning than just following along.

HackUSC Hackathon - Currently ongoing, a collaborative effort across organizations

Last but not the least, HackUSC is a culmination of what all our members have learned and gathered from us and their independent study. This is a hackathon where strategy meets innovation; here, skills are not just tested but also used in building something for the greater good of the community.

When our work extends outwards

Datacamp Donates X GDGoc USC - An ongoing scholarship initiative

One of our initiatives includes giving away Datacamp scholarships worth about $399 (roughly 23,340 PHP) to 100+ students from the university. From there, they have access to career and course certificates to add not only to their resumes and CVs, but skills and technologies in their arsenal.

Our members get access to real-world datasets and courses hand-made by industry experts and authorities in their field.

Tech for Tomorrow - Empowering young minds in the Children’s Shelter of Cebu (2025)

With the incredible support of our members, as well as other students studying at the University of San Carlos, we were able to turn over 111 donations - envelopes containing notebooks, glue, paper, pencils, and more - while partnering with DevConnect Philippines to the learners in the Children’s Shelter of Cebu. This is an initiative we hope to replicate again in the coming terms.

Code for a Cause - November 2025

Code for a Cause was a movement in partnership with 4 other student organizations and Rise Above Foundation to help raise funds and awareness for the victims of Typhoon Tino.

Catch Codey: In partnership with DevConnect Philippines - To be held on March 2026

In partnership with DEVCON - with their recent endorsement by DepED Cebu - we hope to hold Catch Codey!, an initiative where Codey, our mascot, visits schools in each city to teach students that programming and computer science is fun.

Working alongside others

GDGoc-USC is partnered and affiliated with numerous other organizations both within the university and outside of it.

What we can do to help

Progress rarely happens in a vacuum. We bridge the gap between technology and the greater community through the following:

The GDG Network

We are connected to a myriad of different organizations both local and international. We open doors to collaborations and partnership opportunities that transcend normal regional boundaries. This has helped us facilitate introductions, co-host events, and connect our partners with other GDG chapters and tech communities across the country.

Amplified Exposure

With countless events - big and small - held throughout the academic year, the presence of our partners and sponsors are well within reach by our members. We recognize the value our partners bring in helping make these events possible. Our events offer a venue for exposure and engagement, and we do our best to connect you to our community through speaking opportunities, branded activities, booths, and interactive sessions to leave a lasting impression.

Our Roots Within the University

Through our solid presence in the University of San Carlos (USC), our partners can hope to reach a vast and diverse audience of talented and eager students who are actively seeking opportunities to learn and engage.

What we need

We are constantly seeking partnerships to help sustain our initiatives thanks to our nature as a non-profit organization. We are primarily and actively run by university students at the University of San Carlos and we pour a lot of our excess time and energy into turning our events into reality despite our busy lives and hectic schedules. Managed and maintained by a mix of first-years, sophomores, working scholars, OJT trainees, and alumni, we look to the support of the greater community to keep things running.

To sustain and expand what we do, we welcome partnerships in various forms, including:

  • Per-event sponsorships – supporting specific programs, workshops, or competitions
  • Financial contributions – helping cover operational and logistical costs
  • In-kind support – venues, equipment, food, merchandise, printing, or services
  • Contract-based collaborations – co-hosted activities, commissioned projects, or recurring partnerships
  • Promotional exchanges – features, acknowledgments, and social media shout-outs

We’re open to arrangements that align with shared values and mutual benefit. If there’s a way to build something meaningful together, we’re always willing to explore it.
